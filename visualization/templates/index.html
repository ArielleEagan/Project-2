Rationale

As future Data Science and STEM professionals, we wanted to learn more about the job market and earning potential in various available roles. This chart compares the salaries of data scientists at several large tech companies based on years of experience and level of their position. Companies may be toggled on and off to compare only select companies or to look at individual companies information. Order at the top is based on number of entries in the dataset, largest to smallest (L to R).

The source of the data, levels.fyi, aggregates salary information for a variety of tech positions from a wide range of companies utilizing user-submitted information. As we explored the data, we found that this reliance on user input caused inconsistency in much of the data (i.e. L7 and l7 for the same level of position). In addition, each company uses different identifiers for different job levels. As a result of the complexity this adds for cleaning the data, we elected to compare only Data Scientist positions at the five companies with the largest number of entries in the dataset.

Having made this decision, we invested much time in researching the classification systems for each of the five companies, and divided each level of employee into one of three buckets. As much as possible, we attempted to group each companies levels into like buckets.

This is the breakdown of the buckets:

  • Senior Level (large circles):

    • Apple: ICT5
    • Amazon: L6
    • Facebook: IC6
    • Google: L5
    • Microsoft: 64, 65

  • Mid Level (medium circles):

    • Apple: ICT4
    • Amazon: L5
    • Facebook: IC5
    • Google: L4
    • Microsoft: 61, 62, 63

  • Entry Level (small circles):

    • Apple: ICT3
    • Amazon: L4
    • Facebook: IC3, IC4
    • Google: L3
    • Microsoft: 59, 60, 61
